Theater Tech

Discover the world of stage production, lighting, sound, and set design in Theater Technology.

About Theater Tech

Theater Technology (Stagecraft) at Hemet High School immerses students in the creative and technical aspects of live performance. This program is perfect for students interested in the behind-the-scenes magic that brings productions to life.

  • Set Design & Construction: Learn to design, build, and paint scenery using industry-standard tools and materials.
  • Lighting Design: Operate lighting boards, create lighting plots, and explore the impact of color and mood on stage.
  • Sound Design: Set up microphones, speakers, and soundboards; mix audio for live performances.
  • Stage Management: Organize rehearsals, call cues, and coordinate backstage crews for smooth productions.
  • Props & Costumes: Source, create, and manage props and costumes to enhance storytelling.
  • Safety: Practice safe use of tools, electricity, and stage equipment at all times.
  • Live Production Experience: Work on school plays, musicals, assemblies, and community events as part of the tech crew.

Sample Projects

  • Design and build a set for the annual school musical or play
  • Create lighting and sound cues for a live performance
  • Organize and manage backstage operations during a production
  • Construct props and costumes for themed events or competitions

Skills You'll Develop

  • Technical problem-solving and troubleshooting
  • Collaboration and communication with directors, actors, and crew
  • Time management and organization under production deadlines
  • Creative design and artistic expression

Certifications & Real-World Experience

  • Opportunities to earn OSHA-10 safety certification for theater environments
  • Hands-on experience with industry-standard lighting and sound equipment
  • Participation in regional theater festivals and technical competitions
  • Networking with local theater professionals and guest artists

Opportunities & Pathways

  • Participate in school productions, festivals, and competitions
  • Take on leadership roles as crew chief or stage manager
  • Prepare for college theater programs or entry-level jobs in theater, film, or live events
  • Build a portfolio of technical and creative work
