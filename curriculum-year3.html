Year 3: Advanced Systems

Advanced diagnostics, computer systems, and specialized automotive technologies

Course Overview

Year 3 introduces advanced automotive technologies and sophisticated diagnostic procedures. Students will work with computer-controlled systems, fuel injection technology, transmission systems, and advanced electrical components. This year emphasizes independent problem-solving and professional-level diagnostic skills.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Computer-controlled engine management
  • Advanced fuel injection systems
  • Automatic transmission service
  • Air conditioning and climate control
  • Advanced electrical system diagnostics

Learning Objectives

  • Perform advanced computer system diagnostics
  • Service and repair fuel injection systems
  • Diagnose transmission and driveline problems
  • Service automotive air conditioning systems
  • Use professional-level diagnostic equipment
  • Interpret complex wiring diagrams and schematics
  • Develop comprehensive repair estimates

Course Modules

Module 1

Computer Diagnostics

Advanced computer system diagnosis using professional-grade diagnostic equipment.

  • Engine Control Module (ECM) Operation
  • Sensor Testing and Calibration
  • Actuator Testing Procedures
  • Data Stream Analysis
  • Diagnostic Trouble Code Interpretation
  • System Programming and Updates
Module 2

Fuel Injection Systems

Comprehensive study of electronic fuel injection systems and components.

  • Fuel Injector Testing and Service
  • Fuel System Pressure Testing
  • Throttle Body Service
  • Mass Air Flow Sensor Diagnosis
  • Fuel Rail and Regulator Service
  • Fuel System Cleaning Procedures
Module 3

Transmission Service

Automatic transmission operation, maintenance, and diagnostic procedures.

  • Automatic Transmission Operation
  • Fluid and Filter Service
  • Torque Converter Diagnosis
  • Electronic Transmission Controls
  • Transmission Cooler Service
  • Driveline Component Inspection
Module 4

Air Conditioning Systems

Automotive climate control systems, refrigeration principles, and service procedures.

  • Refrigeration Cycle Operation
  • A/C Component Testing
  • Refrigerant Recovery and Recycling
  • Leak Detection Procedures
  • System Performance Testing
  • Climate Control Diagnostics
Module 5

Advanced Electrical

Complex electrical system diagnosis and repair of modern vehicle electronics.

  • Network Communication Systems
  • Body Control Module Diagnosis
  • Advanced Lighting Systems
  • Power Window and Door Lock Systems
  • Security System Operation
  • Electrical Troubleshooting Strategies
Module 6

Professional Practices

Industry standards, customer relations, and business practices in automotive service.

  • Service Writing and Estimates
  • Customer Communication Skills
  • Quality Control Procedures
  • Warranty and Liability Issues
  • Shop Management Basics
  • Continuing Education Requirements

Prerequisites

  • Successful completion of Year 2
  • Intermediate diagnostic skills
  • Electrical system competency
  • Professional work habits

What's Next?

Year 4 focuses on industry certifications, hybrid/electric vehicles, and career preparation for professional entry into the automotive field.
