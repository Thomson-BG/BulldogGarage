Professional Level Training

The Ultimate CTE Program

Designed for beginners. A 4 year program to take you from zero to professional technician, with hands-on experience, industry certifications, and real-world projects.

← Main

What Makes This Program Special?

  • 4-year pathway: Intro to Automotive, Intermediate, Advanced, and Capstone
  • Hands-on shop training with modern vehicles and equipment
  • Industry certifications: Ford ACE, ASE Entry-Level, and more
  • Real-world projects and work-based learning
  • College and career readiness support
  • Professional guest speakers and field trips

Program Structure

Our Professional Level Training is a comprehensive 4-year pathway designed to take students from the basics of automotive technology to advanced diagnostics and repair. Each year builds on the last, ensuring a strong foundation and progressive skill development:

  • Year 1: Introduction to Automotive Technology
  • Year 2: Intermediate Automotive Systems & Service
  • Year 3: Advanced Diagnostics, Electronics, and Drivability
  • Year 4: Capstone: Work-Based Learning & Industry Certification

Certifications & Industry Connections

Students have the opportunity to earn valuable industry certifications, including:

  • Ford Automotive Career Exploration (ACE)
  • ASE Entry-Level Certification
  • SP2 Safety & Pollution Prevention
  • Mitchell ProDemand Access

Student Experience

Students work in a modern automotive shop, using industry-standard tools and diagnostic equipment. The program features:

  • Project-based learning and real vehicle repairs
  • Guest speakers from the automotive industry
  • Field trips to dealerships, trade shows, and technical colleges
  • Opportunities for internships and job shadowing

College & Career Readiness

Graduates are prepared for entry-level employment, advanced training, or college programs in automotive technology, engineering, or related fields. Our staff provides guidance on career pathways, resume building, and interview skills.

Program Outcomes

  • 100% of students complete at least one industry certification
  • Over 80% of graduates pursue further education or employment in automotive or related fields
  • Students regularly participate in SkillsUSA and other competitions
  • Alumni network for mentoring and job opportunities
