Year 4: Professional Preparation

Industry certifications, hybrid/electric vehicles, and career preparation

Course Overview

Year 4 represents the culmination of the automotive program, focusing on industry certification preparation, emerging technologies like hybrid and electric vehicles, and comprehensive career readiness. Students will work independently on complex projects and prepare for immediate entry into the automotive workforce or continued education.

Key Focus Areas:

  • ASE certification test preparation
  • Hybrid and electric vehicle technology
  • Advanced diagnostic procedures
  • Shop management and business practices
  • Career portfolio development

Learning Objectives

  • Prepare for and pass ASE certification examinations
  • Service hybrid and electric vehicle systems safely
  • Perform master-level diagnostic procedures
  • Understand automotive business operations
  • Develop professional career portfolio
  • Demonstrate leadership and mentoring skills
  • Plan career path and continuing education goals

Course Modules

Module 1

ASE Certification Prep

Comprehensive preparation for Automotive Service Excellence certification examinations.

  • ASE Test Categories Overview
  • Engine Performance (A8) Preparation
  • Brakes (A5) Test Preparation
  • Electrical/Electronic Systems (A6)
  • Practice Tests and Review
  • Test-Taking Strategies
Module 2

Hybrid & Electric Vehicles

Cutting-edge training on hybrid and electric vehicle systems and safety procedures.

  • High Voltage Safety Procedures
  • Hybrid System Operation
  • Electric Motor Diagnostics
  • Battery Pack Service
  • Charging System Technology
  • Regenerative Braking Systems
Module 3

Advanced Diagnostics

Master-level diagnostic procedures and complex problem-solving techniques.

  • Complex System Interactions
  • Intermittent Problem Diagnosis
  • Advanced Oscilloscope Usage
  • Network Communication Diagnosis
  • Root Cause Analysis
  • Diagnostic Report Documentation
Module 4

Shop Management

Business aspects of automotive service including management, economics, and operations.

  • Service Department Operations
  • Parts and Inventory Management
  • Labor Time and Pricing
  • Customer Service Excellence
  • Quality Assurance Programs
  • Environmental Compliance
Module 5

Career Portfolio

Development of professional portfolio and career preparation materials.

  • Resume and Cover Letter Writing
  • Interview Skills and Preparation
  • Professional References
  • Certification Documentation
  • Work Sample Collection
  • Career Goal Setting
Module 6

Capstone Project

Independent project demonstrating mastery of automotive technology and professional skills.

  • Project Planning and Management
  • Complex Vehicle Diagnosis
  • Repair Implementation
  • Quality Control and Testing
  • Project Documentation
  • Professional Presentation

Industry Certifications

ASE Certification

Automotive Service Excellence certification in multiple categories including Engine Performance, Brakes, and Electrical Systems.

Industry Recognition

Hybrid/EV Safety

Specialized certification for working safely with high-voltage hybrid and electric vehicle systems.

Future Technology

Equipment Certification

Certification on specific diagnostic equipment and manufacturer-specific tools and procedures.

Technical Expertise

Prerequisites

  • Successful completion of Year 3
  • Advanced diagnostic competency
  • Independent work capability
  • Professional attitude and ethics

Career Opportunities

Graduates are prepared for immediate employment in automotive service, dealerships, specialty shops, or continuation to post-secondary automotive programs.
