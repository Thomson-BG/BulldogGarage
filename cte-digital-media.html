Digital Media

Develop creative and technical skills in digital media, video production, and graphic design.

What You'll Learn in Digital Media

  • Graphic Design: Learn the principles of design, color theory, and typography. Create digital artwork, posters, flyers, and logos using industry-standard tools like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. Students work on real-world projects such as branding for school events or clubs.
  • Video Production & Editing: Plan, script, shoot, and edit videos. Learn camera operation, lighting, sound recording, and video editing with software like Adobe Premiere or Final Cut Pro. Projects may include short films, music videos, commercials, or school news segments.
  • Audio Production & Podcasting: Record and edit audio for podcasts, interviews, and video projects. Add sound effects, background music, and voiceovers using digital audio workstations. Students may produce their own podcast series or audio stories.
  • Animation & Motion Graphics: Design animated graphics, intros, and short films using tools like Adobe After Effects or Blender. Learn frame-by-frame animation, keyframing, and motion design for use in videos, presentations, and social media.
  • Photography & Photo Editing: Master camera settings, composition, and lighting to capture high-quality photos. Use editing software to enhance images, create photo collages, and retouch portraits. Students may cover school events or create photo essays.
  • Web Design Basics: Learn HTML, CSS, and web design principles to build and style simple websites. Explore user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) design, and create personal or project-based web pages.
  • Digital Storytelling & Content Creation: Develop creative stories and multimedia content for digital platforms, including blogs, YouTube, and social media. Combine text, images, audio, and video to engage audiences and communicate messages effectively.
  • Social Media & Digital Marketing: Explore strategies for promoting content, building an online presence, and engaging audiences on platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter. Learn about analytics, content calendars, and digital advertising basics.
  • Media Ethics & Copyright: Understand the legal and ethical responsibilities of digital creators, including copyright, fair use, privacy, and responsible online behavior. Discuss real-world case studies and best practices.
  • Portfolio Development: Build a professional digital portfolio to showcase your best projects, including graphic designs, videos, photos, and web pages. Learn how to present your work for college applications or job opportunities.

Students participate in hands-on projects, collaborate in teams, and use the latest technology to prepare for careers in digital media, communications, and creative industries. The course emphasizes creativity, technical skills, and real-world applications, helping students build a strong foundation for future success.
