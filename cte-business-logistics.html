Business Logistics

Learn the fundamentals of business operations, supply chain management, and logistics.

What You'll Learn in Business Logistics

  • Supply Chain Management: Understand the entire journey of a product, from raw materials to the customer. Learn how companies coordinate suppliers, manufacturers, warehouses, and retailers to deliver goods efficiently and cost-effectively.
  • Inventory Control & Warehousing: Explore how businesses track, store, and manage inventory. Study warehouse layouts, stock rotation, and the technology used to keep products organized and available when needed.
  • Transportation & Distribution: Discover the systems and strategies for moving goods by truck, rail, air, and ship. Learn about route planning, shipping logistics, and how companies ensure on-time delivery.
  • Purchasing & Procurement: Learn how businesses select suppliers, negotiate contracts, and purchase materials or products. Understand the importance of building strong supplier relationships and ethical sourcing.
  • Business Communications & Teamwork: Develop skills in professional communication, both written and verbal. Practice working in teams to solve logistics challenges and complete projects.
  • Information Systems & Technology: Use software and digital tools to track shipments, manage inventory, and analyze logistics data. See how technology improves efficiency and decision-making in logistics operations.
  • Financial Procedures & Economics: Study the financial side of logistics, including budgeting, cost analysis, and understanding how economic trends affect supply chains.
  • Entrepreneurship & Business Planning: Explore how to start and manage a logistics-related business. Learn about business plans, marketing, and innovation in the logistics industry.
  • Principles of Management & Business Law: Gain an introduction to management styles, leadership, and the legal aspects of running a business, such as contracts and regulations.
  • Leadership, Problem-Solving, & Career Development: Build leadership skills, learn to solve real-world logistics problems, and explore career paths in business, supply chain, and operations management.

Students participate in hands-on projects, case studies, and simulations to prepare for careers in logistics, supply chain management, and business operations across all industries.
