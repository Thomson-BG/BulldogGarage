The essentials to ensure you stop.

Stopping Power

Stop with confidence.

Modern braking systems are a blend of mechanical precision and electronic innovation. At their core, disc brakes use calipers to squeeze brake pads against spinning rotors, converting kinetic energy into heat and slowing the vehicle. Drum brakes, though less common on newer cars, operate on a similar principle but use shoes pressing outward against a drum. Both systems rely on hydraulic fluid to transmit force from the pedal to the brakes themselves, ensuring consistent and reliable stopping power.

Maintenance is crucial for optimal brake performance and safety. Brake pads and rotors wear down over time and must be inspected regularly for thickness, scoring, or warping. Neglecting these components can lead to reduced braking efficiency, increased stopping distances, and even complete brake failure. Additionally, brake fluid should be checked and replaced as recommended by the manufacturer, since moisture contamination can degrade its effectiveness and cause corrosion within the system.

Advanced braking technologies, such as Anti-lock Braking Systems (ABS) and Electronic Brakeforce Distribution (EBD), have become standard in most modern vehicles. ABS prevents wheel lockup during hard stops, allowing the driver to maintain steering control, while EBD optimizes brake force between front and rear wheels for balanced stopping. Understanding and maintaining these systems not only enhances safety but also ensures your vehicle performs as designed in emergency situations.
