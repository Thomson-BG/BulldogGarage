First Responder

Prepare for a career in emergency response, public safety, and lifesaving skills.

About the First Responder Pathway

The First Responder pathway at Hemet High School prepares students for fast-paced, rewarding careers in emergency response, public safety, and lifesaving services. This program is ideal for students interested in becoming EMTs, firefighters, police officers, or pursuing other public safety roles.

  • Emergency Medical Response (EMR): Learn the basics of patient assessment, airway management, CPR, bleeding control, and shock management.
  • First Aid & CPR: Gain hands-on training and certification in lifesaving skills used by first responders.
  • Fire Science: Explore fire behavior, firefighting equipment, and fire prevention strategies.
  • Law Enforcement: Understand police procedures, criminal justice, and the role of law enforcement in the community.
  • Disaster Preparedness: Learn how to plan for and respond to natural and man-made disasters.
  • Communication & Teamwork: Develop skills to work effectively with other responders and agencies during emergencies.
  • Legal & Ethical Issues: Study confidentiality, consent, and professional conduct in emergency situations.
  • Simulated Scenarios: Practice with mock emergencies, triage, and rescue operations to build real-world skills.

Skills You'll Develop

  • Critical thinking and rapid decision-making in high-pressure situations
  • Effective communication and teamwork with diverse agencies
  • Leadership and responsibility in emergency care
  • Hands-on emergency care and rescue techniques
  • Professionalism and ethical responsibility

Opportunities & Pathways

  • Earn certifications in First Aid, CPR, and EMR
  • Participate in ride-alongs, field trips, and guest speaker events with local fire, EMS, and law enforcement agencies
  • Prepare for college programs in emergency medical services, fire science, law enforcement, or public safety
  • Explore careers as EMTs, paramedics, firefighters, police officers, or emergency managers
