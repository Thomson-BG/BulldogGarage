Sports Medicine

Gain knowledge in sports injury prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation for athletic careers.

About Sports Medicine

The Sports Medicine pathway at Hemet High School introduces students to the dynamic world of athletic healthcare, injury prevention, and rehabilitation. This program is ideal for students interested in athletic training, physical therapy, exercise science, or healthcare careers.

  • Anatomy & Physiology: Study the musculoskeletal and other body systems essential for movement and athletic performance.
  • Injury Prevention: Learn taping, bracing, and conditioning techniques to help reduce the risk of sports injuries.
  • Injury Assessment: Develop skills to evaluate and recognize common sports injuries and determine appropriate care.
  • First Aid & CPR: Gain emergency response skills and opportunities for certification in First Aid and CPR.
  • Rehabilitation: Explore therapeutic exercises and modalities used in the recovery process.
  • Nutrition & Wellness: Understand the impact of diet, hydration, and lifestyle on athletic performance and recovery.
  • Sports Psychology: Learn the basics of mental health, motivation, and performance in athletes.
  • Clinical Experience: Assist with school athletic teams, work with certified trainers, and participate in hands-on labs and simulations.

Skills You'll Develop

  • Critical thinking and problem-solving in real-world scenarios
  • Effective communication and teamwork with coaches, athletes, and healthcare professionals
  • Hands-on medical and therapeutic skills
  • Professionalism and ethical behavior in healthcare settings

Opportunities & Pathways

  • Work as a student athletic trainer for school sports teams
  • Earn certifications in First Aid and CPR
  • Prepare for college programs in kinesiology, athletic training, physical therapy, or sports medicine
  • Explore careers in healthcare, fitness, and sports performance
