Anatomy/Nursing

Explore the Anatomy and Nursing pathway, preparing students for careers in healthcare and medical sciences.

About the Anatomy & Nursing Pathway

The Anatomy and Nursing pathway at Hemet High School provides students with a comprehensive introduction to the world of healthcare and medical sciences. This program is designed for students interested in pursuing careers in nursing, medicine, physical therapy, or other allied health professions.

  • Human Anatomy & Physiology: Explore the structure and function of the human body, including all major organ systems, tissues, and cells.
  • Medical Terminology: Learn the language of healthcare to communicate effectively in medical settings.
  • Health Science Foundations: Gain an understanding of healthcare careers, ethics, safety, and the healthcare environment.
  • Patient Care Skills: Practice basic nursing skills such as measuring vital signs, assisting with patient hygiene, and maintaining infection control.
  • CPR & First Aid Certification: Receive training and certification in emergency response and life-saving techniques.
  • Clinical Experience: Participate in simulated labs and, when available, real-world internships in local hospitals, clinics, or nursing homes.
  • Professionalism: Develop communication, teamwork, and ethical decision-making skills essential for healthcare professionals.

Student Experiences

  • Practice hands-on skills in a simulated hospital lab environment
  • Shadow healthcare professionals and participate in field trips to local medical facilities
  • Engage in health science competitions and HOSA (Health Occupations Students of America) activities
  • Collaborate on group projects focused on real-world healthcare challenges

Certifications & Opportunities

Students in this pathway may earn industry-recognized certifications such as Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) and CPR. Graduates are well-prepared for further study in college healthcare programs or for entry-level positions in the medical field.

Why Choose Anatomy & Nursing?

  • Hands-on learning with real medical equipment and simulations
  • Opportunities for clinical experience and internships
  • Preparation for college and healthcare careers
  • Guidance from experienced instructors and healthcare professionals

Career Pathways After Graduation

  • Registered Nurse (RN) or Licensed Vocational Nurse (LVN)
  • Medical Assistant or Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)
  • Physical Therapist or Occupational Therapist
  • Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)
  • Pre-medical or pre-health college programs
  • Other allied health professions
