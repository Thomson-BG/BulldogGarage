Year 1: Foundation

Building the fundamentals of automotive technology and safety

Course Overview

Year 1 serves as the foundation for your automotive journey. Students will learn essential safety procedures, basic automotive systems, and develop fundamental skills required for advanced coursework. This year emphasizes hands-on learning combined with theoretical knowledge to ensure a solid understanding of automotive principles.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Workshop safety and tool identification
  • Basic engine operation and components
  • Automotive fluid systems
  • Fundamental electrical concepts
  • Professional communication skills

Learning Objectives

  • Demonstrate proper safety procedures in automotive workshop environments
  • Identify and properly use basic automotive tools and equipment
  • Understand fundamental engine operating principles
  • Perform basic maintenance tasks under supervision
  • Recognize major automotive systems and their functions
  • Apply basic electrical circuit concepts to automotive applications
  • Communicate effectively using automotive terminology

Course Modules

Module 1

Safety & Shop Orientation

Essential safety protocols, personal protective equipment, and shop organization fundamentals.

  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
  • Shop Safety Rules and Procedures
  • Emergency Procedures
  • Tool Safety and Handling
  • Environmental Hazards
  • Fire Safety and Prevention
Module 2

Tool Identification & Usage

Comprehensive introduction to automotive tools, measurement devices, and equipment operation.

  • Hand Tool Identification
  • Measuring Tools and Precision
  • Power Tool Operation
  • Lifting Equipment Safety
  • Tool Maintenance and Care
  • Shop Equipment Overview
Module 3

Basic Engine Systems

Introduction to internal combustion engines, major components, and basic operating principles.

  • Engine Block and Components
  • Piston and Connecting Rod Assembly
  • Crankshaft and Camshaft Operation
  • Valve Train Components
  • Four-Stroke Engine Cycle
  • Engine Measurements and Specifications
Module 4

Fluid Systems Overview

Basic understanding of automotive fluids, their purposes, and maintenance requirements.

  • Engine Oil Properties and Functions
  • Coolant System Basics
  • Brake Fluid Characteristics
  • Power Steering Fluid
  • Transmission Fluid Basics
  • Fluid Level Checking Procedures
Module 5

Tire & Wheel Service

Hands-on experience with tire inspection, rotation, and basic wheel service procedures.

  • Tire Construction and Types
  • Tire Pressure Monitoring
  • Tire Rotation Patterns
  • Wheel Balancing Basics
  • Tire Wear Pattern Analysis
  • Safety Considerations
Module 6

Basic Electrical Concepts

Fundamental electrical theory applied to automotive systems and components.

  • Voltage, Current, and Resistance
  • Ohm's Law Applications
  • Series and Parallel Circuits
  • Automotive Battery Basics
  • Basic Circuit Testing
  • Electrical Safety Procedures
Module 7

Basic Maintenance Procedures

Hands-on practice with routine maintenance tasks and service intervals.

  • Oil Change Procedures
  • Filter Replacement (Oil, Air, Fuel)
  • Fluid Level Checks
  • Battery Maintenance
  • Basic Inspection Procedures
  • Service Documentation
Module 8

Professional Skills

Development of communication, teamwork, and professional behavior in automotive settings.

  • Automotive Terminology
  • Customer Service Basics
  • Teamwork and Collaboration
  • Time Management
  • Work Order Documentation
  • Professional Appearance and Behavior

Assessment Methods

Practical Skills (40%)

Hands-on demonstrations of safety procedures, tool usage, and basic maintenance tasks.

Written Exams (30%)

Tests covering theoretical knowledge, safety procedures, and automotive terminology.

Projects (20%)

Individual and group projects demonstrating understanding of automotive systems.

Participation (10%)

Class participation, attendance, and demonstration of professional behavior.

Prerequisites

  • Interest in automotive technology
  • Basic math and reading skills
  • Physical ability to perform shop tasks
  • Commitment to safety protocols

What's Next?

Upon successful completion of Year 1, students advance to Year 2, where they will build upon these fundamentals with more advanced automotive systems and diagnostic procedures.
